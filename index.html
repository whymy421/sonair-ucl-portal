About the Lab

The UCL Robotics Lab focuses on remote teleoperation, sim2real transfer, and networked robot control. We investigate how network latency and jitter affect the fidelity of commands transmitted between simulation environments and physical hardware.

We are open to collaboration on federated benchmarking, cross-institutional teleoperation experiments, and shared dataset curation for the SONAIR network.

Interpretation

Network stability. The mean RTT of ~55 ms with a range of 14–401 ms indicates generally responsive conditions, but with significant outlier spikes. The P95 value shows that 5% of measurements exceed a much higher threshold, suggesting intermittent congestion on the UCL–Nottingham link.

Command latency. The histogram clusters tightly around 20–40 ms for the majority of commands — acceptable for non-safety-critical teleoperation. However, a long tail beyond 100 ms means some commands experience delays that could cause visible desynchronisation between operator input and robot response.

Sim2real implication. In simulation, commands execute instantaneously. In the real setup, variable latency introduces a timing gap. A trajectory planned at 50 Hz assumes each command arrives within 20 ms, but the network delivers with a standard deviation of ~25 ms. The physical robot's trajectory will diverge from the simulated one, particularly during high-speed motions.

Session events. The audit log shows a burst of link_degraded rejections near t=420 s, correlating with RTT spikes in the time-series. Network instability directly impacts teleoperation reliability.

Sim2Real Gap Metric — Temporal Fidelity Index

Challenge 1: A novel metric to quantify the simulation-to-real timing gap for networked teleoperation.

We propose the Temporal Fidelity Index (TFI) — a composite metric that captures how faithfully a real networked teleoperation system reproduces the timing assumptions of its simulation counterpart. Existing sim2real metrics focus on positional or visual accuracy; TFI targets the temporal dimension.

Definition

TFI = (1 − Dnorm) × (1 − Jnorm) × (1 − Lrate)
  • Dnorm — normalised mean excess delay = mean(RTT) / max(RTT). Measures average delay relative to worst case.
  • Jnorm — normalised jitter = std(RTT) / max(RTT). Captures timing unpredictability.
  • Lrate — packet loss rate = lost / total. Measures data loss.

Why It Matters

For teleoperation, timing fidelity is often more critical than positional accuracy — delayed commands accumulate into trajectory divergence. A robot trajectory planned in simulation at 50 Hz assumes each command arrives within 20 ms, but the real network introduces variable delays that compound over time.

Adoption Across SONAIR

TFI can be computed for any teleoperation session that logs RTT and packet loss. Each campus node could publish its TFI alongside raw data, enabling cross-institutional comparison. Over time, a TFI benchmark could identify bottleneck links and establish minimum fidelity thresholds for safety-critical applications.
